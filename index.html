Plan outlook glossary

Definitions for the key terms used in the Plan outlook panel.

Lifestyle resilience

Comfort level
The annual spending level the plan treats as a comfortable lifestyle target. Spending below this level suggests a meaningful reduction in lifestyle.
Minimum level
The minimum annual spending level the plan treats as acceptable for essential living costs.
Worst cut
The largest reduction in annual spending required relative to target spending in the modelled outcomes.

Plan health

Initial withdrawal rate
First-year household spending funded from the portfolio divided by the starting portfolio value.
State pension reliance
The share of total lifetime spending funded by the State Pension rather than portfolio withdrawals.
Portfolio dependence
The share of total household spending funded by portfolio withdrawals rather than pensions or other non-portfolio income.

Portfolio outcomes

10th percentile ending
A weaker-than-average ending portfolio outcome. Only 10% of simulations finish below this level.
90th percentile ending
A stronger-than-average ending portfolio outcome. Only 10% of simulations finish above this level.
Weak-case depletion year
The year in which the portfolio runs out in weaker simulated outcomes, if depletion occurs.

Plan risks

Worst spending shortfall
The largest gap between target spending and actual spending in any year of the simulation.
Years with spending shortfall
Number of years where the plan does not fully meet target spending.
Years below comfort spending level
Number of years where actual spending falls below the comfort spending level.

Before you start

Plan your retirement with clarity

Before you begin, calculate the total value of your investments by gathering your current balances and asset types across pensions, ISAs, and taxable accounts. You will also need your expected income from work, as well as State Pension, dividends, and any other sources.

Your savings are likely spread across various accounts and investment types. For example, your pension typically combines equities for potential growth, as well as bonds or gilts for stability. To see your specific mix, check your provider’s breakdown for your personal asset allocation.

How this tool supports your plan

This tool models how your specific mix of income and investments behaves over time to identify potential pressure points. It will:

Consolidate your income, spending, and investments into a single timeline.
Simulate how shifting market conditions affect your long-term outcomes.
Recalibrate spending automatically to keep your plan on track.
Identify exactly when your target income may become unsustainable.
