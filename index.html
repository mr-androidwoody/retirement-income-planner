Plan outlook glossary

Definitions for the key terms used in the Plan outlook panel.

Plan health

Spending sustainability score
A summary score showing how durable the plan looks overall. Higher scores suggest the plan is more resilient under the model assumptions.
Initial withdrawal rate
First-year household spending divided by the starting portfolio.
Median final withdrawal rate
The middle ending withdrawal rate across the simulated outcomes.
Portfolio dependence
The share of total household spending funded by portfolio withdrawals rather than pensions or other income.

Plan setup

Simulations run
Number of Monte Carlo paths used to test the plan under different return sequences.
Years modelled
Length of the retirement plan being projected.
Starting portfolio
Total investable portfolio value at the start of the simulation.
Total household spending
Total spending delivered across the full plan horizon, before splitting by income source.

Portfolio outcomes

Median ending portfolio
The middle ending portfolio value across all simulated outcomes.
10th percentile ending
A weaker-than-average outcome. Only 10% of simulations finish below this level.
90th percentile ending
A stronger-than-average outcome. Only 10% of simulations finish above this level.
Total withdrawals
Total amount drawn from the portfolio across the full plan.

Plan risks

First spending shortfall year
The first year in which actual spending falls below target spending.
Worst spending shortfall
The largest gap between target spending and actual spending in any year.
Years with spending shortfall
Number of years where the plan does not fully meet target spending.
Total state pension income
Total state pension income received across the full plan horizon.
